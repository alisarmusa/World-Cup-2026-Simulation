    Wins to trophy

    First-ever 48-team World Cup, across USA, Canada and Mexico. The new Round of 32 shields the big sides but opens a knockout path for outsiders.

    Europe on top — but fragile. France lead the model with Spain, England and Portugal close. Yet R32 claimed two giants: Germany and Netherlands both out on penalties.

    Disruptors are real. Haaland's Norway reached a first modern R16; Morocco knocked out the Dutch; co-hosts Canada and Mexico made history. Japan pushed Brazil to the 96th and fell.

    Argentina lean on Messi. He passed Klose, hit a record 20th WC goal, and leads the Golden Boot — but needed 111 mins past Cape Verde. Durability over 7 games is the question.

    R32 done. Paraguay stunned Germany, Morocco beat the Dutch, Spain swept Austria 3–0, Portugal edged Croatia late, Belgium escaped Senegal 3–2. Sixteen remain — four wins to the trophy at MetLife, July 19.
